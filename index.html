What Is Uranium?

Uranium is a naturally occurring metallic element found in rocks, soil, and water. It was discovered in 1789 by German chemist Martin Heinrich Klaproth. It is known for being heavy, faintly radioactive, and important in modern energy science.

Basic Properties

  • Symbol: U
  • Atomic Number: 92
  • Appearance: Silvery-grey metal
  • Natural Isotopes: U-238, U-235, U-234

Common Uses

Uranium is used primarily in:

  • Nuclear fuel for electricity generation
  • Scientific research
  • Geological dating techniques
  • Certain types of glass and ceramics for coloring

Safety & Environmental Notes

Uranium is radioactive and chemically toxic in concentrated forms. Natural levels in soil and rock are generally low and not hazardous. Handling of concentrated uranium materials is regulated and must be done by trained professionals. This website provides only general, non-technical scientific information.
